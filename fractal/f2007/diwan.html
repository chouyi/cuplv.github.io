Managing Perturbation

Whenever we measure a computer system, we perturb its behavior. This perturbation has the potential to drastically affect our understanding of the system's behavior.

This talk describes a methodology for detecting perturbation and applies it to two different measurement approaches: (i) overall profiles, which measure end-to-end performance (e.g., total number of cache misses) and (ii) temporal correlations between performance metrics (e.g., correlation of IPC and cache misses). Using the SPEC CPU2006 CINT benchmarks, we explore how the various parameters of these two approaches impact perturbation. Our results show that for either of these approaches even a small measurement overhead (e.g., less than 2% increase in instruction count) can drastically perturb a system.

Amer Diwan, University of Colorado at Boulder
