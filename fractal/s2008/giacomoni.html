Supporting Fine-grain TLP with Selective Timesharing

The industry wide shift to parallel architectures is a serious problem for sequential applications. Application developers can no longer rely on increases in transistor count and fabrication technology to increase performance without their intervention. The question today is, "how do we continue to increase the performance of sequential applications on parallel platforms?" We believe that fine-grain thread level parallelism (TLP) is a viable approach for many applications on general-purpose multicore/multiprocessor machines given: 1) a low overhead software-only core-to-core communication primitive (our previous work), and 2) the ability to control timing jitter between threads. (In this context, we operationally define fine-grain TLP as 0.5 to 5 times the overhead of an empty interrupt handler per unit-of-work, e.g., approximately 100-1000 nanoseconds).

Under reasonable loads interrupts, including non-I/O interrupts, were found to have an observable effect on user-space applications implementing fine-grain TLP. This talk presents our initial work on using Selective Timesharing to control operating system related jitter thereby allowing for the effective use of fine-grain TLP without sacrificing the benefits of a general purpose parallel processing platform.
