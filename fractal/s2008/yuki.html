Performance Modeling of the IBM Cell Broadband Engine Using Neural Networks

Tiling is commonly used to optimize scientific computations. Performance modeling to narrow down the search space is a technique used to help find the best tile sizes. It is difficult to analytically model the performance of the IBM Cell Broadband Engine due to its characteristics. We used neural networks to derive models that predict the best tile sizes for matrix multiplication on the Cell. The performance of the predicted tile sizes were higher than 90% of the observed maximum for most of the problem sizes we tested.
