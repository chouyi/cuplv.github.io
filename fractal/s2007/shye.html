Run-time Compilation Techniques to Enable Multi-Core Systems

Emerging multi-core processor designs create a new, unparalleled computing paradigm capable of advancing scientific areas, including medicine, data mining, biology, physics, and earth sciences. These designs are the cornerstone of all future general-purpose and high-performance computing systems. However, a crisis is developing because the trends in hardware technology have advanced far ahead of the advances in software technology. Performance improvements will only be possible with advances in software to fully utilize the multi-core hardware. Realizing the full potential of future multi-core machines in the presence of emerging trends in power/energy control, temperature regulation, and transistor reliability requires a run-time compilation system capable of adapting thread-level behavior to changing applications needs as well as the execution environment change. We examine several characteristics and services enabled by a multi-core run-time compilation system. The highlights of the system include the construction of a run-time system approach to transient fault tolerance and the development of an on-line performance modeling and profiling framework for directing multi-core resource allocation and optimization.

Alex Shye, University of Colorado at Boulder
