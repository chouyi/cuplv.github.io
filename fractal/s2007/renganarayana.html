Posynomials and Optimal Tiling : A Positive Match!

Optimal tiling---selecting the "best" tile sizes---is an important and well widely studied problem in high performance parallel as well as sequential implementations. Though widely studied, current solutions suffer from two important limitations (i) Inextensibility---solution methods are strongly coupled to the cost models and any extensions to cost models make the solution inapplicable; (ii) Scalability---the solutions address only a single-level of tiling and do not scale to multiple levels of tiling. Based on the fundamental mathematical properties of the functions used in optimal tiling models, we propose a framework which provides an extensible as well as scalable solution to the optimal tiling problem. Our framework is made of two components: a modeling formalism based on a special class of polynomials called posynomials and a generic convex optimization solver. The former is used for specifying optimal tiling models and the later is used as single generic solution method. We have successfully reduced optimal tiling models proposed by different authors for a variety tiling contexts---parallelism, caches, registers---into models in our framework.
