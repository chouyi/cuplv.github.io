Development Support for Concurrent Threaded Pipelining

Single-core performance increases have stalled. To increase available cycles, microprocessor designers have shifted to chip-multiprocessor (CMP) designs. These designs will provide tens to hundreds of processing cores per system that may be asymmetric in performance and heterogeneous in capability. Harnessing these resources is critical as these cores may individually be less powerful than existing single core solutions. Unfortunately, the additional cores provided by CMPs may remain idle because most applications lack data-parallelism and task-parallelism is unlikely to saturate future CMP designs. The systems community needs to rethink how software is developed to fully utilize CMPs.

We propose that programming languages and system libraries be extended to explicitly support Concurrent Threaded Pipeline (pipeline- parallel) organizations. Pipeline-parallel organizations differ from traditional data- and task-parallelism in that most inter-data dependencies are automatically handled. However, harnessing any potential performance improvements requires careful management of the underlying computer architecture to achieve the necessary core-to- core communication performance. In this talk we will discuss the potential performance improvements of Concurrent Threaded Pipelining and the language support necessary to support it at both the compiler and programmer levels.
