The Massively Parallel Computing Revolution


Ross Cunniff(Nvidia Corporation) Keynote - Fractal Workshop 2009

Abstract Single-threaded and low-thread-count computational performance has reached a plateau. It is widely recognized that future performance improvements will come from massively parallel systems. Several different parallel processing models have been proposed; most have failed. This session explores NVIDIA's CUDA proposals and explains their success factors via several case studies.

Biograph Ross Cunniff graduated with dual degrees in Computer Science and Mathematics from New Mexico State University. For fifteen years, Ross was a lead engineer at Hewlett-Packard Company specialized in compilers and graphics. During this time, Ross was awarded several graphics patents. In 2001, Ross became lead engineer and director at NVIDIA Corporation, investigating multi-GPU functional and performance. Ross directs the transformation of new consumer applications to GPU computation.
